Vintage Treasures at Bang Bang 70s, Hong Kong

  • Celine
  • Gucci
  • HongKong
  • Vintage
  • 5 comments

Just returned from our first trip ever to Hong Kong – what a fantastic place! It was a work trip, but we managed to cover a very wide spectrum of fairly unique experiences:

crassness at the Hong Kong Rugby Sevens
a day spent scouting locations for a friend’s upcoming film in a small fishing village on Lan Tau Island
the expansiveness of the Hong Kong Exhibition and Convention Center (our feet are still hurting, but now we’re working with French tanneries that have been around since 1680 and make beautiful leathers.. more on that later!)
Dim Sum at two competing recommendations for best Dim Sum in Hong Kong (Fu Sing and Dynasty, both in Wan Chai – we’ll leave it to you to decide which is better)
Drinks at the gorgeous Café Gray at the Upper House Hotel
Dinner at Yardbird – delicious Japanese food, a great buzz, and delicious Ginger Ale infused beer
A broad sampling of Japanese whiskeys at 001, an underground drinking den
The list goes on and on
We didn’t really have too much time to shop, unfortunately. But on the last day, after snapping up as much beautiful leather as we could from willing suppliers at the fair and with a few hours left to kill before heading back to the airport, we decided to see what Hong Kong shopping was all about.

Custom tailors and Sham Shui Po aside, there’s one standout experience that really floored us – a small vintage shop called Bang Bang ‘70s (just off Hollywood Road, on Aberdeen Street).

You have to ring a bell outside a metal door, and venture up a tiled stairway into this tiny den of a shop… and you’re immediately hit with nostalgia. Old soda bottles, plastic figurines, briefcases, bags, board game boxes, sunglasses, ties, clocks, phones, dresses, leather jackets, shoes – there was just stuff everywhere. On shelves, lining the floor, in these crazy cabinets, hanging from the ceiling (and a ridiculous old fan from Holland). And it was all beautiful, vintage (not retro “styled), and mostly in pristine condition. There was certainly a lot of product on display, but somehow it made sense:
